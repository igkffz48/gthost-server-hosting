## Performance and Network Features

The technical foundation is where GTHost stops looking like a budget host and starts looking like serious infrastructure. Here's what's actually under the hood.

**Storage:** NVMe and SAS SSD on VPS; enterprise Samsung/Micron SSDs and Seagate HDDs on dedicated servers. NVMe makes a real, measurable difference for database-driven workloads — WordPress admin gets snappier, page generation drops noticeably, and I/O-bound batch jobs finish faster.

**Bandwidth:** Unmetered on VPS and most dedicated configs, ranging from 300 Mbps up to 10 Gbps. Unmetered doesn't mean infinite — it means no overage charges if you push the rated port speed. That's a meaningful distinction for high-traffic sites and APIs.

**Network backbone:** GTHost runs its own AS and IP allocations, uses Juniper Networks routing exclusively, and operates a 100GE network infrastructure. They peer with major Tier-1 carriers including GTT, Cogent, and Zayo, plus exchange points like DE-CIX (Frankfurt), Equinix IX Paris, IX-Denver, and Detroit IX. /64 IPv6 is available on request, and BGP/BYOIP is supported on monthly dedicated servers when ROA and route objects are in place.

**SLA:** The network uptime target is 99.999%, with service-credit compensation when SLA conditions are met (capped at the monthly recurring fee). Some marketing pages state "100% network uptime SLA" with 12:1 credit — the precise terms are in the SLA document. Either way, the credits are issued as account credit, not cash refunds.

**Deployment speed:** VPS and instant dedicated servers come online in 5–15 minutes after payment, 24/7. The automated system handles Linux OS installation (CentOS, Ubuntu, Debian, Fedora) without human intervention. According to GTHost, the instant dedicated inventory contains over 4,000 servers across the 22 locations.
